Humans By Lonely Planet

Our Challenge:

Lonely Planet is looking for some top-level ideas for a digital companion piece to fit nicely into their ecosystem and be used pre, during and post travel. This new product has to help travelers find accommodation, restaurants, local sites etc., as well as allow users to share their experiences with friends and fellow travelers. Target audience are backpackers with low/medium travel budget ages between 18 and 34.

Eco-system Analysis:

- Books, e-books and magazines — richness in content, however some editions are outdated.
- Mobile Apps — the majority are still travel guides which results in a large number of duplication in terms of content among different Apps.
- Website — serves as a showcase of the above digital products, in addition provides online booking.

User interviews & Survey:

Strategies for people to use the app:

- Pre Travel: Use in-app search function to get the list of local guides and book one.
- During Travel: Enable geo-location service to find who’s near and connect right away.
- Post Travel: Add reviews, ratings to help other people finding their perfect local guide.

Our solution:

Humans by Lonely Planet takes the control out of the hands of Lonely Planet and place it into face to face human interaction.
It facilitates Lonely Planet as an established brand, however the content is no longer dictated by the editors.
